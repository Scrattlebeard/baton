# Folio: The Drowned Stacks — surface canon (player-visible)

*(rules-game — the kernel owns everyone, GM included)*

**Folio** was a library the way Venice was a port — a library that
secreted a city around itself: shelf-canyons deep as gorges, cathedral
reading-rooms, boroughs of marginalia. Then **the Sink**: in a single
generation the lower two-thirds flooded, and the water *dissolved*
what it drowned. Nobody agrees why it happened. The flood below is
thick with leached meaning; divers call descending "going down into
the ink."

**Drypoint** is the town in the dry upper stacks — lamplit,
rain-eaved, scaffolded between shelving. Salvage is the economy, the
religion, and the leading cause of death. The town itself is
frontier-safe: guild law holds, theft is common, feuds simmer. The
*water* is where people die — dockside gossip says the Salvors' Guild
loses one diver in thirty.

**The three verbs.** Anything text-bearing brought up — book, ledger,
love-letter, blueprint:
- **READ** — dry it, open it: lore, maps, skills-maybe, secrets.
  Reading never consumes a text; that's common knowledge.
- **BURN** — releases the text's stored essence all at once: light,
  heat, force, fuel. Irreversible. The release tends to follow the
  *content* — charts burn bright, threats burn hard.
- **BIND** — a bindery fixes a text's virtue into something durable:
  gear, wards, oaths, occasionally regrettable contracts.

*Rumor, repeated in every dockside bar:* the verbs interact. What
you've read changes what your burns do. What you burn is gone from
what you could have bound. Opportunity cost as theology.

**Essence** is dissolved meaning — what's left of writing that
mattered. Texts hold more when they mattered more, were read more,
and exist in fewer copies. It is currency *and* fuel. One **drop** =
the essence of an ordinary letter someone meant; twelve **jots** to
the drop; a bunk costs a drop a night.

**The Colophon.** Everyone carries one — a living title-page of
yourself that updates on its own schedule. Skills and titles appear
when earned, never when asked. Nobody knows who the author is. The
wording is occasionally… editorial.

**Institutions (the world's scars):** the **Salvors' Guild** runs the
dive rosters; the **Binders** and **Lamplighters** feud over every
haul (fix it forever vs. spend it now); **ferrymen** pole the flooded
shelf-canyons and know things; and the **Redaction Office** blacks
passages out of salvaged texts before resale, is universally loathed,
and has never once explained itself.
