# Neon Tithe — surface canon (player-visible)

*(story-crawler — the GM owns the map, you own the path)*

**Meterwork** is the city that solved municipal finance by metering
everything. Streetlight bills by the lumen-minute. Cooled air by the
breath. Memory storage by the month, with late fees assessed in
forgetting. The whole apparatus is the **Tithe**, and every citizen
carries a **Meter** — wrist-fused, tamper-scarred, counting your
balance in the only currency that ever mattered: **seconds**. Time on
the meter is money, is rent, is breathable air on the hot floors. When
your Meter hits zero it doesn't kill you. It just stops vouching for
you, and Meterwork is a very tall city with very few railings for the
unvouched.

**The powers that be (and bill):**
- **The Assessors** — the audit priesthood. Grey coats, perfect
  posture, authority to re-price anything, including apologies.
- **The Recirculators** — repo crews for chrome, organs, and
  outstanding ambience. Polite. Punctual. Uninsured against.
- **The Grey Grid** — the off-meter barrio wedged into the city's
  service floors: barter, static, unlicensed noodles, and the job
  board where people like you find work that pays in raw seconds.
- **The static cults** — worship the hiss between stations; claim the
  Tithe has a heartbeat and that they've heard it skip. Mostly
  harmless. Mostly.

**You** are a runner. Your Meter is low enough that the display has
started rounding sympathetically. The Grey Grid board posts jobs most
nights — courier work, escort work, the occasional entry into places
whose doors bill by the attempt. Jobs branch, choices close doors, and
the city remembers. Somewhere above the smog line, someone always
knows more about your job than you do; the trick is billing *them* for
the difference.

**Tone:** a romp — fast, chrome-bright, funny right up until it
isn't, never longer than one floor's fall from dangerous.
