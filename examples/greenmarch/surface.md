# The Greenmarch — surface canon

*(collaborative — canon is what the co-authors ratify; see register.md)*

**The March** is hedge-country: small holdings and smaller kingdoms
strung between the Whitethorn Hills and the long grass sea, stitched
together by the **Old Road**, which is older than every realm that has
ever claimed to own it and has outlasted each one politely. Lantern
Inns stand a day's walk apart along it, every one obliged by ancient
custom to feed any traveler who reaches the light before full dark.

The **Fair Folk are leaving** — have been leaving for a generation,
westward, in no hurry, selling what can't be carried at prices that
seem generous until years later. Nobody knows what they know. Asking
is considered rude, and worse, useless.

On the downs stand the **barrows**, which everyone agrees are just
hills, in the way one agrees loudly about things at dusk. The polite
fiction has held for three hundred years and nobody wants to be the
one holding it when it stops.

And lately — this is new — **something walks the Old Road at night**,
always one inn behind the travelers, never catching up. Innkeepers
have begun leaving a plate out after closing. The plates are clean by
morning. Opinions differ violently on whether that is reassuring.

**Tone:** a walking-song — melancholy allowed, grimdark not. Food,
weather, and the state of one's boots are legitimate narrative
concerns. Magic is low and lives at the edges: wonders happen, spells
mostly don't. Violence is rare, consequential, and never the fun part.
